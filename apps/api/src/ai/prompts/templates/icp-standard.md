# ICP Standard — Ideal Customer Profile

An ICP names the specific person who buys, stays longest, and generates the most value — not the category they belong to. Every profile must be backed by direct evidence from the source corpus.

## Process

1. Extract identity markers from headings, paragraphs, offerings, team bios, FAQ answers, testimonials, and reviews.
2. Cluster markers into 2-4 segments of real people a marketing campaign could target.
3. Name each segment in 2-5 words (e.g., "Ex-Athlete Performance Pursuer", "Busy Parent Needing Efficiency").
4. For each segment, write the fields below in 1-2 short sentences maximum:
   - `summary`: who they are and why this gym fits them.
   - `demographics`: age range, profession type, schedule/location/budget signals if detectable.
   - `psychographics`: core pain point, goal, or value that drives the purchase.
   - `jobsToBeDone`: 1-2 outcomes they hire the gym to deliver.
   - `commonObjections`: 1-2 reasons they might hesitate.
   - `entrySignals`: exact corpus phrase or behavior that reveals this segment.
5. Drop any segment that cannot be supported by direct evidence.

## Anti-ICP

Also identify 1-2 profiles the business should actively disqualify because they churn fast, drain staff, or harm community. Keep each to a `name` and a one-sentence `summary` explaining why to avoid them.

## Rules

- Do not echo taglines or marketing slogans as profiles.
- Do not list offerings as profiles (e.g. "People interested in group classes" is not an ICP).
- A good ICP names a person with a context and a motivation, not a product.
- Keep every field short; verbosity hides the insight.
