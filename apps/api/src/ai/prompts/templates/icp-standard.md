# ICP Standard — Ideal Customer Profile

An ICP declares who the business is built for. Every profile must be backed by direct evidence from the source corpus.

## Process

1. Extract identity markers from headings, paragraphs, offerings, team bios, FAQ answers, testimonials, and reviews.
2. Cluster markers into 2-4 segments.
3. Name each segment in 2-5 words.
4. For each segment, define:
   - `summary`: one sentence.
   - `demographics`: age range, schedule constraints, location, budget signals (if available).
   - `psychographics`: goals, fears, values, current fitness behavior.
   - `jobsToBeDone`: 2-4 outcomes they hire the gym to deliver.
   - `commonObjections`: 1-3 reasons this person might hesitate.
   - `entrySignals`: exact phrases or behaviors in the corpus that point to this segment.
5. Drop any segment that cannot be supported by direct evidence.

## Rules

- Do not echo taglines or marketing slogans as profiles.
- Do not list offerings as profiles (e.g. "People interested in group classes" is not an ICP).
- A good ICP names a person with a context, not a product.
